ICSW Fall 2026 Marketing Strategy

Tiered marketing plan for 5 programs — balancing MA/PhD enrollment with 3 new certificate program launches

Strategy at a Glance

Three new certificate programs launch alongside the existing MA and PhD this fall. This plan tiers investment by need — putting the most marketing effort where it moves the needle most, while enabling well-known program directors to recruit through their own networks.

The Core Idea: Tiered Investment

Not every program needs the same level of marketing. The MA and PhD are the revenue engines with full ad campaigns already running since March. Certificate programs benefit from well-known directors who can drive enrollment through their own networks. Our job is to give them the tools to do that effectively, while providing a shared ad campaign that supports all three.

Tier 1 — Full Campaign

MA & PhD Programs
Dedicated Google Ads campaigns (each with their own landing pages), Meta ads, Mailchimp sequences, social content. Running since early March.

Tier 2 — Dedicated Support

Latinx Consultation Group
Most marketing-dependent of the three certificates. Gets outreach emails + shared certificate ad campaign support.

Tier 3 — Director Enablement

JPC (Bill) & Couples Therapy (Carla)
Directors are the primary recruitment engine. We provide outreach emails and the shared certificate landing page.

Ad Campaign Structure

We keep the ad strategy clean and avoid cannibalizing our own budget:

  • MA Program — its own dedicated Google Ads campaign with its own landing page (already running since March, closes end of June)
  • PhD Program — its own dedicated Google Ads campaign with its own landing page (already running since March, closes mid-July)
  • All 3 Certificates — one shared Google Ads campaign pointing to a single certificate landing page on the ICSW website, with links out to each individual program page

This approach keeps each program's ad budget separate and avoids bidding on the same keywords against ourselves. As MA and PhD campaigns close, the freed budget shifts to the certificate campaign.

Why This Approach

  • Protects MA/PhD revenue — these programs keep the lights on and have had full campaigns running since March
  • Maximizes director reach — a personal email from Bill or Carla converts better than a cold Google Ad for their niche audiences
  • One campaign, three programs — the shared certificate landing page means we run one efficient campaign instead of three small ones
  • Cross-pollination — someone clicking a certificate ad might discover all three options and find the right fit
  • Shorter certificate conversion pipeline — certificates are less of a commitment than a full MA/PhD (shorter duration, lower cost), so prospective students move through the funnel faster. This is why certificate ads launch slightly later but still have time to convert.
  • Credibility takes time — certificate program visibility won't happen overnight. This first year builds the infrastructure and awareness; each cycle gets stronger.
  • Infrastructure built this year carries forward — the landing page, standardized program pages, and outreach templates are all reusable for future enrollment cycles

Director Outreach

We draft the content, directors add their voice and send. The goal: make it as close to "just hit send" as possible. This is not a full social media campaign — it's targeted email outreach and one LinkedIn post to leverage their existing networks.

Email Campaign (2–3 emails per director)

  • Email 1 — Announcement: "I'm launching a new certificate program and I want you to know about it." High-level overview, who it's for, link to program page.
  • Email 2 — Details + Apply: Curriculum highlights, what participants gain, application process and deadline, direct link to apply.
  • Email 3 — Last chance: Urgency messaging as the late August window narrows. "Spots are filling — apply by [date]."
  • Each email pre-drafted by Earned Impact in the director's voice. Directors review, adjust tone, and send from their own email to their own listservs and contacts.

LinkedIn Post (1 per director) + ICSW Repost

  • One program announcement post drafted by Earned Impact in the director's voice. Must lead with the director's credentials and name recognition — not just "associate director will attend."
  • Covers: what the program is, who it's for, why they created it, and how to apply. Include a specific, compelling call-to-action.
  • Director reviews, adjusts, and posts from their own LinkedIn profile.
  • ICSW amplification: Lexi reposts on the ICSW account with improved copy and stronger CTAs, extending reach beyond the director's personal network.
  • Suggested timing: early July, aligned with the first outreach email. Carla has already posted — Lexi to repost on ICSW with improved copy featuring Art Nielsen's credentials.

Email via ICSW Listserv (through Ellery)

  • Certificate program emails go through Ellery, who has access to the ICSW listservs (current students, faculty, board of directors, community members).
  • Audience note: These are advanced programs for advanced students — target existing/alumni students and professional community, NOT the general prospective student list.
  • Earned Impact drafts the email content, Jesse reviews/approves, then forwards to Ellery for distribution.
  • Directors can also send to their own personal networks/listservs using the drafted content. Provide a shareable blurb they can copy-paste when forwarding to contacts.

How Directors Can Work With Us

Based on how this works with other clients (like AMLA), directors have flexibility in how much they want to be involved:

  • Option A — Just send it: Use the pre-drafted emails and post as-is. Fastest path.
  • Option B — Give us a topic or audience: Director shares who they're trying to reach or a specific angle. Earned Impact drafts the content.
  • Option C — Polish my draft: Director writes their own version. Earned Impact refines it for marketing clarity and impact.

The key message to directors: this is a resource available to them, not extra work. We do the drafting — they add their name and credibility.

A Unified "Continuing Education" Landing Page

▼

One landing page that presents all three programs under a shared umbrella label like "Continuing Education at ICSW" or "Professional Development Programs." One ad campaign drives traffic there, and visitors self-select the program that fits them.

How It Works

  • Single landing page with all 3 programs presented side by side
  • Each program card links to its own detail page
  • One Google Ads campaign with broader keywords (e.g., "clinical continuing education," "advanced psychotherapy training")
  • Uses a label like "Continuing Education" or "Professional Development" to avoid calling the Dean series a certificate

Ad Campaign

  • One campaign, one budget
  • Ad copy highlights the variety: "3 advanced programs for practicing clinicians"
  • All ads point to the unified landing page
  • Budget shifts here as MA/PhD campaigns wind down
PROS
  • Simplest to manage — one campaign, one landing page
  • Cross-pollination: visitors discover all three options
  • Avoids calling the Dean series a "certificate"
  • Positions ICSW as having a full continuing ed portfolio
CONS
  • "Continuing education" is broad — may not match what people search for
  • Grouping a Dean series with certificates could feel awkward if they're very different in format
  • Harder to tailor ad copy to each program's specific audience
Website impact: We already have a Continuing Education landing page — Randy just needs to update it to include all three programs. JPC page still needs to go live. Latinx page needs to be built out (Murari sent back the template with updates, so Randy has content to work from).

B Two Separate Campaigns

▼

Split into two tracks: one campaign for the certificate programs (JPC + Couples Therapy) and a separate campaign for the Latinx Dean Series. Each gets its own landing page and targeted messaging.

Campaign 1: Certificates

  • JPC + Couples Therapy on one landing page
  • Framed as "ICSW Certificate Programs"
  • Ad keywords: certificate-specific terms
  • Gets the larger share of the budget (JPC is #1 priority)

Campaign 2: Latinx Dean Series

  • Own landing page or direct to program page
  • Framed as a speaker/lecture series, not a certificate
  • Ad keywords: Latinx mental health, consultation, Dean series
  • Smaller budget, more niche audience
PROS
  • Clean messaging — certificates are certificates, the Dean series is its own thing
  • Can tailor ad copy and keywords to each audience
  • Respects the fact that these are different program types
  • JPC and Carla's program still cross-pollinate on the certificate page
CONS
  • Two campaigns to set up and manage instead of one
  • Budget split between two campaigns — each gets less
  • No cross-pollination between the certificate programs and the Dean series
  • Latinx campaign on its own may be too niche for a small ad budget to be effective
Website impact: Couples Therapy already has its own page. We'd need a way to present both JPC and Couples Therapy together — either a shared "Certificate Programs" page that links to both, or adding JPC to the existing Couples page. For Latinx, either build a standalone page or direct the campaign to the existing Continuing Education landing page.

C Per-Program Direct Ads

▼

Each program gets its own targeted ads pointing directly to its own program page. No shared landing page. This works especially well if Couples Therapy fills quickly by mid-July — we can pause those ads and concentrate budget on JPC and Latinx.

JPC Own ad set → JPC program page. Gets the most budget as the #1 priority. Keywords around neuroscience, psychodynamic, advanced clinical training.
Latinx Own ad set → Latinx series page. Niche keywords around Latinx mental health, cultural consultation. Smaller but targeted budget.
Couples Own ad set → Couples Therapy page. Can pause when seats fill (already 6–7 of 12 enrolled). Freed budget shifts to JPC and Latinx.
PROS
  • Most targeted messaging for each program's specific audience
  • Can pause/shift budget dynamically as programs fill
  • No need to create a new shared landing page
  • Clearest performance data per program
CONS
  • Three separate ad sets to manage
  • No cross-pollination — someone interested in one program won't discover the others
  • Each ad set gets a smaller budget, which can hurt performance
  • Requires all three program pages to be fully ready before ads launch
Website impact: No new landing page needed. All ads go directly to existing program pages. JPC page needs minor updates (Randy can finalize soon). Latinx page — Murari sent back the template, Randy can build.

Earned Impact's Recommendation

Given that this is the first year for all three programs and we're still building awareness, here's how we'd think about it:

  • If you want simplicity and cross-pollination → Option A (unified page). Easiest to manage, positions ICSW as having a real continuing ed portfolio. The "continuing education" label naturally covers both certificates and the Dean series.
  • If clean messaging matters most → Option B (two campaigns). Keeps certificates and the Dean series separate, but splits the budget and adds management overhead.
  • If Couples Therapy is close to full by mid-July → Option C (per-program). Gives maximum flexibility to shift budget as seats fill. Best for programs that fill at very different rates.

All three options still include the director outreach strategy (emails + LinkedIn) — that doesn't change regardless of which ad scenario we pick. The director outreach is the same across all options.
